Camera Degradation Detection

Embedded C99

Real-time degradation watchdog for an externally mounted automotive camera.
ARM Cortex-M0 · No FPU · No dynamic memory · <2KB RAM · Integer arithmetic only

What This Module Does

Purpose

Runs 10 times per second on a 30fps camera stream (every 3rd frame). Examines the image and raises an alarm when the lens is unreliable due to environmental conditions.

This is detection only — no image correction, no dehazing, no filtering.

Classification Outputs

CLEAN FOG FROST DIRT OBSTRUCTION UNKNOWN

Confidence: uint8_t (0–255)
Hysteresis: 5 frames to enter degraded · 15 frames to clear

Detection Pipeline

1 · Input Frame
Raw uint8 grayscale, row-major, W×H bytes. No header, no format. Every 3rd frame is processed.
↓
2 · Single-Pass Feature Extraction (stride 4)
One pass over the image, sampling every 4th pixel in x and y. All 8 features computed simultaneously — no second pass.
laplacian_var global_contrast histogram_spread global_mean occlusion_score obstruction_score cell_mean_variance temporal_mad
↓
3 · Dual-Timescale Baseline EMA
Learns what "normal" looks like for this specific camera and scene.
Fast EMA (α = 1/16) · Slow EMA (α = 1/256)
60-frame warmup — alarms suppressed until baseline is stable.
↓
4 · Ratio Computation
Each feature is compared to its baseline:
ratio = (current × 128) / (baseline + 1)
128 = no change · <128 = dropped · >128 = rose
↓
5 · Decision Tree Classifier
Hard-coded priority order resolves ambiguities between types. See diagram →
↓
6 · Hysteresis State Machine
Prevents flickering between states.
Enter degraded: 5 consecutive frames
Clear to clean: 15 consecutive frames
↓
Output: DegradationResult
classification · confidence (uint8) · feature snapshot

Classification Decision Tree

1
DIRT
occlusion_score HIGH
+ cell_mean_variance HIGH
Dark blobs present + frame is spatially non-uniform
2
OBSTRUCTION
obstruction_score HIGH
+ cell_mean_variance HIGH
+ occlusion_score LOW
Any-brightness blob (ice, tape) — not dark mud
3
FROST
laplacian_var LOW + global_mean HIGH
+ histogram_spread VERY LOW
+ cell_mean_variance LOW
Bright veiling + uniform blur + histogram skewed high
4
FOG
global_contrast LOW + laplacian_var LOW
+ global_mean NEAR BASELINE
+ cell_mean_variance LOW
Global contrast reduction + uniform across frame
5
CLEAN
None of the above triggered
6
UNKNOWN
Contradictory feature combination

The 8 Features Extracted Per Frame

# Feature Name Type How It's Computed What It Detects
1 laplacian_var uint8 3×3 Laplacian kernel applied at sampled pixels; variance of results Sharpness — drops for fog and frost; roughly normal for dirt in unaffected regions
2 global_contrast uint8 Track min + max of sampled pixels; (max−min)×256/(max+min+1) Michelson contrast — drops when fog or frost reduces dynamic range
3 histogram_spread uint8 8-bin histogram; span between 5th and 95th percentile bins Dynamic range — very low for frost (histogram collapsed to high end); low for fog
4 global_mean uint8 Sum of all sampled pixels ÷ count Key frost/fog discriminator — HIGH for frost (bright veil), mid for fog
5 occlusion_score uint8 Count of 4×3 grid cells with low internal variance AND dark mean Dark-blob detector — HIGH only for mud/dirt
6 obstruction_score uint8 Count of 4×3 grid cells with low internal variance (any brightness) Any opaque obstruction — bright tape, ice, sticker, or dirt
7 cell_mean_variance uint8 Variance of the 12 cell mean values across the frame Spatial uniformity — HIGH for dirt (local blobs), LOW for fog/frost (uniform degradation)
8 temporal_mad uint16 Mean Absolute Difference vs 16×12 thumbnail of previous frame; EMA smoothed Inter-frame motion — low + stable when scene texture is locked by degradation

C Concepts to Understand (for non-C developers)

.h vs .c files — Header = contract (what exists). Source = implementation (how it works). Any file that wants to use our module just includes the header.
Static allocation — Every buffer declared once at compile time as a fixed array. No surprise memory requests at runtime. The MCU always knows exactly what RAM is in use.
No floats → bit shifts — x >> 4 = divide by 16. x * 128 / baseline = a ratio centered on 128. All integer, all fast.
uint8_t overflow — 255 + 1 = 0 (wraps). Every accumulator must be sized to never overflow its range. We document every risk point.
Pointers — const uint8_t *frame passes the image's memory address, not a copy. frame[y*W + x] reads pixel at (x, y).
